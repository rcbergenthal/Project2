We at 4 the music heads were conceived by a group of music aficionados with a penchant for puns, Four the Music-Heads began as a small record store with big dreams of spreading the joy of music to the masses. From vinyl to digital, Four the Music-Heads is dedicated to celebrating the diverse and ever-evolving world of music, offering curated playlists, artist spotlights, and concert recommendations for music lovers of all tastes. Our mission is to inspire a lifelong love affair with music, fostering discovery, connection, and a deep appreciation for the transformative power of sound.

Weekly Spotlights

Dive into our Weekly Music Spotlight, where we unearth the most electrifying sounds and hidden gems across genres that demand your attention. Each week, our passionate music curators scour the musical landscape to bring you an in-depth look at emerging artists, groundbreaking albums, and the tracks that shaoed the sonic landscape.

Back Home Again • John Denver • 1974

"Back Home Again" solidified John Denver's status as a leading folk-country artist, celebrating rural American life and environmental themes with his characteristic warmth and sincerity. The album's title track became one of Denver's most beloved songs, embodying his romanticized vision of home and connection to nature.

Apples and Oranges • Bob Pyle • 2004

Bob Pyle's Apples and Oranges is, overall, puzzleing. Who is this for? one may ask. No one knows, probably only pyle himself. However, "Gentle Creatures" and "I've Been Restored" are shockingly tender and almost religious thematically. Also, my dad used to sing those two songs to me when i was little.

Car Button Cloth • The Lemonheads • 1996

The album, featuring the hit cover of Simon and Garfunkel's "Mrs. Robinson," demonstrated The Lemonheads' ability to merge raw emotional authenticity with catchy, accessible musical arrangements.

Voulez Vouz • ABBA • 1979

ABBA at the height of the disco era, showcasing their ability to blend sophisticated pop arrangements with danceable rhythms. The album represents a sophisticated evolution of their sound, featuring lush production and exploring themes of romantic complexity and nightlife.

Pet Sounds • The Beach Boys • 1966

The record's complex harmonies, innovative production techniques, and deeply personal lyrics about love, isolation, and emotional vulnerability profoundly influenced generations of musicians, including The Beatles.

Paul's Boutique • Beastie Boys • 1989

The record is a dense, kaleidoscopic journey through pop culture references, intricate musical collages, and witty, rapid-fire lyrics that challenged and expanded the boundaries of hip-hop.

Some Girls • The Rolling Stones • 1978

"Some Girls" marked a critical comeback for the Rolling Stones, blending punk and disco influences with their classic rock sound. The album features iconic track "Beast of Burdern"

Deja Vu • Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young • 1970

"Déjà Vu" represented a pinnacle of the late 1960s folk-rock movement, combining the supergroup's exceptional harmonies with politically charged and deeply personal songwriting. The album features classic tracks like "Woodstock" and "Our House," capturing the complex social and emotional landscape of a transformative era in American music.
